感電｜In the Current 

2026

3m * 3m * 3m

電器設備、電感元件、金屬、電子電路、喇叭

Electrical equipment, inductive components, metal, electronic circuits, speakers

Every creature experiences the world differently. For example, birds can see a wider range of colors than we can, and bats can hear much higher frequencies. While we feel absolutely nothing from the electromagnetic fields around us, to a bee, they are an invisible storm vibrating every hair on its body. To a shark, they feel like the heartbeat of an unseen prey.

Today, we have filled the sky and earth with invisible electrical currents and signals, yet we remain completely unaware of them. To other creatures, humans seem like strangers in a noisy world we built ourselves.

In an era hard to leave electronic products, people inevitably spend long periods immersed in various electromagnetic signals. These weak, continuous waves fill the air like a transparent background. People usually focus only on the content and function carried by the signals, rather than the signals themselves as an existing reality. These electromagnetic activities keep vibrating, overlapping, and flowing outside our sense of perception, creating a silent but non-stop noise.

In the Current uses coils to detect electromagnetic waves from everyday appliances and amplifies them into sounds we can actually hear. By interacting with the piece, you can play with these hidden sounds and create your own rhythms of light and audio, giving you a fresh look at the technological signals woven into our daily lives.

每種生物所能感受到的波都有所不同，譬如，人類能看見的光譜比鳥類要來的窄，能聽見的音域也比蝙蝠要來的低頻。人類對日常周遭的電磁場毫無感覺，但對於蜜蜂而言，那可能是全身毛髮都在隨之震顫的隱形風暴；對鯊魚而言，是一隻看不見的巨大獵物在不斷跳動。

當我們用無形的交流電與訊號填滿了整片天空與土地，自己卻一無所知、一無所感。在牠們眼裡，人類對這座由自己一手打造的喧囂世界，竟是如此陌生。

在一個難以離開電子產品的時代，人們無可避免地長時間浸身於各種電磁訊號之中。這些在空氣中瀰漫的微弱持續波動，卻多半被視為透明的背景，人們關注的往往只是訊號所承載的內容與功能，而非訊號本身作為一種正在發生的存在。這些電磁活動在人的感知範圍之外持續顫動、交疊與流動，形成一種無聲卻不間斷的喧囂。

作品《感電》透過線圈感測電器產生的電磁波，並將其放大為人耳可聽見的聲響。觀眾能透過作品操作，將這些日常物件的電磁波聲響組合成自由的聲光節奏，進而重新檢視這些遍布在我們日常生活中的科技訊號。

技術與設計說明 Technical and Design

When we were kids, adults always warned us: "Don't leave electronics by your bedside, or you won't sleep well." Inspired by this childhood memory, the artwork In the Current recreates a small bedside scene, using the artist's own everyday items like a nightlight, charger, alarm clock, fan, and power strip. By using coils and inductors, the piece captures the faint electromagnetic waves these objects emit while running. An operational amplifier (Op-amp) then boosts these signals into sounds we can actually hear, allowing us to explore the invisible waves behind our daily technology.

To make the sound more engaging, the artwork includes a custom-built sequencer. Instead of letting the electromagnetic waves play as continuous, monotonous noise, the sequencer turns them into musical notes and rhythms. People can freely adjust the speed and beats, which also changes the flashing patterns of the lights. This allows people to remix the hidden sounds of these bedside electronics into a fun, playful piece of music.

小時候大人總會叮嚀：不要把電子產品放在床頭，以免影響睡眠。作品《感電》延伸自這段童年記憶，收集了作者床頭的電子產品，包含夜燈、充電器、鬧鐘、風扇與延長線等，將其布置成一個小型的床頭場景於展覽現場。作品透過線圈與電感，感測這些物件運作時散發的微弱電磁波，再藉由運算放大器（Op-amp）將訊號放大至人耳可聽見的聲頻範圍，藉此探索日常科技背後的無形訊號。

同時，作品結合了自製的序列器（Sequencer），使電感捕捉到的電磁聲響不再只是持續播放的單調噪音，而是能被視為一種聲音元素。透過序列器編排出的各式節奏，觀眾可以自由操控節拍的增減與速度，同時也觸發燈光的閃爍頻率，將這些床頭電子產品的電磁波聲響，重組為充滿趣味的音樂。
