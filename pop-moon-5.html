震痕｜Vibration and Traces

2026

4m * 4m * 3m

五金零件、馬達、拾音器、板金、電子元件

Hardware parts, motors, audio pickups, sheet metal, electronic components

In 2011, during an American football game in Seattle, over 60,000 fans cheered and jumped so hard for a touchdown that a nearby seismograph detected a 30-second vibration. This event was named the "Beast Quake." It not only made the touchdown player, Marshawn Lynch, famous in earth science but also changed how we think about an "Artificial earthquake"—showing it doesn't just come from intentional destruction.

"Artificial earthquake" means a ground vibration caused by human activity rather than nature, usually from energy development or scientific tests. However, as the global population grows, unintentional artificial earthquakes are also appearing. Even our daily walking and jumping can accidentally create vibrations strong enough to be measured, or even cause real damage to our surroundings.

Vibration and Traces captures the subtle movements and footsteps we often ignore in daily life. By amplifying these tiny vibrations and noises through electronic circuits, the energy is turned into power for a wall-drilling machine. During the exhibition, this artwork gathers vibrations from the environment to leave permanent marks in the space, turning invisible human movements into visible physical damage.

2011年在西雅圖的一場美式足球賽中，六萬多的球迷因為達陣得分集體狂歡跳動，竟然讓設置在球場附近的地震儀偵測到了持續30秒的震動，此地震被稱為「野獸地震（Beast Quake）」。這個事件成功讓達陣的球員Marshawn Lynch在地球科學界留下一席之位，同時，對於「人造地震」一詞的認知也不再僅限於人類刻意為之的破壞行為。

「人造地震」意指由人類活動引起而非自然產生的地殼震動，常見於能源開發或科學實驗。隨著人類數量加增，非刻意產生的人造地震也開始形成。僅僅是人日常中的移動、跳躍也會在無意中產生足以成為地震的震動，甚至實質的對環境造成破壞。

作品《震痕》嘗試捕捉人們日常中容易忽略的行走與移動，將其引起的微小震動與噪音透過電路放大，轉化為鑽牆的機械裝置動力來源。本作品在展覽期間匯聚環境中的震動，於空間中留下不可逆的痕跡，將無形的人類行為震動，具象化為實質的環境破壞。

Vibration and Traces consists of 15 wall-drilling installations. Each one is equipped with a custom vibration sensor and an amplifier circuit. We use audio pickups as sensors, placing them flat against floors or walls where they can easily catch sounds and tiny vibrations from the environment.

When the sensor catches a vibration, the signal is sent to an operational amplifier (Op-amp) to be amplified and adjusted. This step sets a "trigger threshold" and filters out background noise. This ensures that even the quietest sounds are accurately captured without accidental triggers, allowing the signal to steadily power the motors.
The mechanical parts of the 15 installations are mainly made of metal and mounted on the wall. Each one uses a DC motor with a grinding tip. A built-in spring structure keeps pushing the grinding tip firmly against the wall. When the motor is triggered by a vibration signal and starts to spin, it slowly wears away at the wall, drilling a hole that keeps getting deeper.

The artwork is displayed in a dark room. When a vibration is detected, it not only triggers the drilling but also lights up the installation, creating feedback through sound, light, and shadow. At the same time, the dust left behind from the drilling naturally becomes part of the artwork's visual story.

作品中包含有十五座的鑽牆裝置，每座裝置皆搭配有自製的震動感測器及震動放大電路。作品中使用拾音器作為震動感測器，並將感測器平貼於地面、牆面等易於捕捉環境與空間聲音和震動訊號的位置。

拾音器所捕捉到的震動訊號將經由運算放大器（Op-amp）的放大處理與基準電壓調校（Reference Voltage Calibration）。此調校機制主要用於設定系統的觸發門檻（Threshold Setting）與訊號偏置調整（DC Bias Adjustment），一方面確保微弱的聲音訊號能被精準轉譯，另一方面藉由控制靈敏度，避免環境雜訊造成誤觸，使訊號能穩定且具層次地驅動帶有鑽頭的直流電機。

在機械結構方面，裝置以金屬為主要材質並裝設於牆面上，利用直流馬達搭配砂輪磨頭執行鑽孔動作。透過裝置內的彈簧結構，磨頭能持續受到推力並緊抵牆面，當直流電機受到震動訊號驅動並轉動時，便會在牆上緩慢的磨損出一個孔洞並持續加深。
作品展示在暗室中，當偵測到震動時，除了驅動裝置鑽牆外，裝置本身帶有燈光亦會亮起，形成帶有聲音與光影的震動回饋。同時，裝置在鑽牆過程中所產生的粉塵遺留，也自然地成為作品視覺與敘事的一部分。
