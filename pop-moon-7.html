無感滯留｜Insensible Retention  

Just because we do not notice something does not mean it did not happen. It is like how we always live in humidity, temperature, and airflow—cycles of condensation, dripping, and evaporation keep happening at the edge of our senses. Through tools, we catch these weak feelings that our bodies miss, making these changes stay so we can see them.

“Insensible Retention” keeps turning moisture from the air into water drops, trying to show how tiny changes can build up into something we can feel. Even though the water drops will eventually dry up and go back into the air, the machine gives them a short moment to be noticed.

未曾意識到的事物，不代表它未曾發生。如同人始終浸泡於濕度、溫度與氣流之中，一次次凝結、滴落，再蒸發，不斷在感知的邊界發生。我們藉由工具，攔截那些被身體忽略的微弱感知，使原本無感的變化得以滯留。
作品不斷凝結空氣中的水氣，嘗試製造將極微小的狀態匯聚至有感的過程。水滴終將蒸發回到空氣中，卻得以在作品機制中短暫被感知。
